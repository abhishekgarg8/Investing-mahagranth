# Warren Buffett - In Their Own Words

As of: 2026-06-10T10:27:06Z

## Quote Provenance Note

This file favors primary Buffett venues: Berkshire shareholder letters, the Berkshire Owner's Manual, Buffett Partnership letters, annual-meeting transcripts, and official or transcript-grade interviews. Widely circulated quote-aggregator lines were excluded unless this run could tie them to a specific original venue. Direct quotes are deliberately short; the surrounding annotation carries the interpretation.

## Quotes by Theme

### Owner Mindset and Alignment

1. "Although our form is corporate, our attitude is partnership." - Buffett's basic shareholder contract: Berkshire is public in form but partnership-like in norms ([Berkshire Owner's Manual, 1996](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/ownman.pdf)).

2. "We eat our own cooking." - Alignment is not a slogan; Buffett and Munger expected their own wealth to move with outside shareholders' wealth ([Berkshire Owner's Manual, 1996](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/ownman.pdf)).

3. "We select our marketable equity securities in much the same way we would evaluate a business." - The 1977 letter is the cleanest early statement that public stocks should be analyzed as businesses, not as price marks ([Berkshire 1977 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/1977.html)).

4. "Our favorite holding period is forever." - The famous 1988 line was about high-quality holdings that continue to perform, not a command to keep every mistake ([Berkshire 1988 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/1988.html)).

5. "If you aren't willing to own a stock for ten years, don't even think about owning it for ten minutes." - Buffett's time-horizon filter is meant to prevent pretending that trading is business ownership ([Berkshire 1996 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/1996.html)).

6. "Charlie and I are not stock-pickers; we are business-pickers." - Late-career Buffett still framed marketable stocks as fractional business ownership ([Berkshire 2021 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2021ltr.pdf)).

7. "We are understanding about business mistakes; our tolerance for personal misconduct is zero." - Berkshire's decentralized culture tolerates honest operating errors but not trust violations ([Berkshire 2022 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2022ltr.pdf)).

### Business Quality, Moats, and Valuation

8. "A truly great business must have an enduring 'moat'." - The 2007 letter turns the moat metaphor into an operational filter: defend high returns on capital over time ([Berkshire 2007 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2007ltr.pdf)).

9. "A moat that must be continuously rebuilt will eventually be no moat at all." - Buffett rejects businesses whose advantage depends on constant reinvention rather than durable structure ([Berkshire 2007 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2007ltr.pdf)).

10. "Growth is always a component in the calculation of value." - Buffett's 1992 letter rejects a false split between value and growth investing ([Berkshire 1992 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/1992.html)).

11. "It was far better to buy a wonderful business at a fair price." - Buffett credits Munger with pushing him beyond pure cigar-butt bargain hunting ([Berkshire 2012 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2012ltr.pdf)).

12. "Monthly or yearly movements of stocks are often erratic." - Buffett's 2014 retrospective distinguishes market marks from intrinsic-value progress ([Berkshire 2014 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2014ltr.pdf)).

13. "Berkshire's two-pronged approach to capital allocation gives us a real edge." - Buffett links the public-stock portfolio and operating-company earnings into one permanent-capital machine ([Berkshire 2016 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2016ltr.pdf)).

### Temperament, Market Prices, and Risk

14. "Be fearful when others are greedy, and be greedy when others are fearful." - In the 2006 letter this was tied to catastrophe insurance, then generalized to financial markets ([Berkshire 2006 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2006ltr.pdf)).

15. "Run your business as if it were the only asset your family will own over the next hundred years." - Buffett's instruction to operating managers captures Berkshire's preferred time horizon ([Berkshire 2004 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2004ltr.pdf)).

16. "What is smart at one price is dumb at another." - The 2011 letter applies this to both acquisitions and repurchases; price changes the decision ([Berkshire 2011 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2011ltr.pdf)).

17. "Financial strength that is unquestionable takes precedence over all else." - Buffett's buyback rule is subordinate to Berkshire's ability to meet obligations in stress ([Berkshire 2011 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2011ltr.pdf)).

18. "Games are won by players who focus on the playing field." - The 2013 farm and real-estate essay tells investors to study productive assets rather than scoreboards ([Berkshire 2013 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2013ltr.pdf)).

19. "Forming macro opinions ... is a waste of time." - Buffett is not saying macro never matters; he is saying most forecasts are not actionable edge ([Berkshire 2013 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2013ltr.pdf)).

20. "Speculation is most dangerous when it looks easiest." - Written after the internet bubble, this is Buffett's warning against confusing narrative ease with investment edge ([Berkshire 2000 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2000pdf.pdf)).

### Candor, Mistakes, and Reporting

21. "The CEO who misleads others in public may eventually mislead himself in private." - The Owner's Manual makes candor a managerial self-protection device, not just a disclosure virtue ([Berkshire Owner's Manual, 1996](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/ownman.pdf)).

22. "I believe in establishing yardsticks prior to the act." - Buffett's partnership letters insisted that performance be judged by pre-agreed standards, not after-the-fact stories ([Buffett Partnership Letters, 1962](https://www.ivey.uwo.ca/media/2975913/buffett-partnership-letters.pdf)).

23. "I will not abandon a previous approach whose logic I understand." - This early partnership line explains Buffett's resistance to style drift when his method lagged ([Buffett Partnership Letters, 1967](https://www.ivey.uwo.ca/media/2975913/buffett-partnership-letters.pdf)).

24. "I would rather prep for a colonoscopy than issue Berkshire shares." - Buffett's post-Dexter and post-General Re aversion to stock issuance became almost absolute ([Berkshire 2016 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2016ltr.pdf)).

25. "Every up and down movement of the stocks it owns" - Buffett used the 2019 letter to argue that GAAP earnings can become noisy when unrealized equity moves hit net income ([Berkshire 2019 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2019ltr.pdf)).

26. "Our airline position was a mistake." - The 2020 meeting transcript shows Buffett naming a COVID-era error plainly and promptly ([2020 Berkshire Annual Meeting Transcript](https://s3.amazonaws.com/static.contentres.com/media/documents/5c882ab5-3aaf-4f3d-ba33-d105bd25cc58.pdf)).

27. "I make many mistakes." - Buffett's late letters still pair Berkshire's extraordinary record with repeated error admission ([Berkshire 2021 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2021ltr.pdf)).

### Berkshire Structure, Scale, and America

28. "Float is wonderful - if it doesn't come at a high price." - Insurance float is valuable only when underwriting cost stays below alternative funding cost ([Berkshire 2003 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2003ltr.pdf)).

29. "The babies being born in America today are the luckiest crop in history." - Buffett's optimism is rooted in productivity and compounding, not in a denial of social problems ([Berkshire 2015 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2015ltr.pdf)).

30. "The American Tailwind." - Buffett's phrase for the broad national backdrop that amplified Berkshire's record ([Berkshire 2018 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2018ltr.pdf)).

31. "We are lucky - gloriously lucky - to have that force at our back." - The 2018 letter explicitly credits national context alongside investor skill ([Berkshire 2018 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2018ltr.pdf)).

32. "Berkshire will never prefer ownership of cash-equivalent assets over the ownership of good businesses." - Cash is a reserve and option, not the preferred long-term asset ([Berkshire 2024 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2024ltr.pdf)).

33. "The problem with the investment business is that things don't come along in an orderly fashion." - At the 2025 meeting, Buffett explained why Berkshire can hold huge cash without abandoning opportunism ([2025 Berkshire Annual Meeting Transcript](https://steadycompounding.com/transcript/brk-2025/)).

## Annotated Index of Primary Materials

### Core letter collections

- [Buffett Partnership Letters, 1957-1970](https://www.ivey.uwo.ca/media/2975913/buffett-partnership-letters.pdf) - Best single source for early Buffett: partnership yardsticks, workouts/generals/controls, bear-market expectations, fee alignment, and the decision to close the partnership when opportunity narrowed.
- [Berkshire Shareholder Letters archive](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/letters.html) - Official index for the public-company corpus. Use this before quote aggregators; many famous Buffett lines are distorted when stripped of year and context.
- [Berkshire Owner's Manual](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/ownman.pdf) - The durable constitution for Berkshire's culture: partnership attitude, owner alignment, conservative leverage, candor, acquisition discipline, and communication norms.

### High-value annual letters by year

- [1977 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/1977.html) - Early statement that marketable equities should be evaluated like whole-business acquisitions.
- [1988 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/1988.html) - Source for the "favorite holding period" line and the permanent-holdings discussion around Coca-Cola, GEICO, and Washington Post.
- [1989 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/1989.html) - Essential for mistakes, the institutional imperative, and Munger's influence on buying wonderful companies at fair prices.
- [1992 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/1992.html) - Best source for Buffett's argument that value and growth are joined, not opposing schools.
- [1996 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/1996.html) - Primary source for circle of competence, ten-year ownership, portfolio concentration, and the two-course investing curriculum.
- [2000 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2000pdf.pdf) - Post-bubble source on speculation, chain-letter economics, quality control on Wall Street, and why changing technology can defeat valuation certainty.
- [2001 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2001pdf.pdf) - Useful for property/casualty insurance economics, float cost, and underwriting discipline.
- [2003 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2003ltr.pdf) - Good float primer and acquisition-culture source, especially around insurance promises and unusual risk capacity.
- [2004 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2004ltr.pdf) - Index-fund benchmark candor, operating-manager trust, and the instruction to run a business as if it were the family's only asset.
- [2006 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2006ltr.pdf) - The fear/greed line appears in catastrophe-insurance context, making it more than a stock-market aphorism.
- [2007 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2007ltr.pdf) - One of the best philosophy letters: great/good/gruesome businesses, See's Candies, moats, low capital needs, and limits of management heroics.
- [2008 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2008ltr.pdf) - Crisis-year letter covering mark-to-market losses, Graham's price/value lesson, derivatives, and several admitted investment errors.
- [2011 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2011ltr.pdf) - Repurchase discipline, purchasing-power definition of investing, beta versus real risk, and Berkshire's cash floor.
- [2012 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2012ltr.pdf) - Munger's fair-price/wonderful-business teaching, Heinz structure, and the dividend-policy essay.
- [2013 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2013ltr.pdf) - Buffett's farm and New York real-estate essay, especially useful for separating asset productivity from market quotation.
- [2014 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2014ltr.pdf) - Fifty-year retrospective on Berkshire's market value, intrinsic value, insurance, and institutional evolution.
- [2015 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2015ltr.pdf) - American productivity optimism, the cost of Berkshire textile/Dexter mistakes to workers, and long-term economic context.
- [2016 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2016ltr.pdf) - Share issuance aversion, index-fund fee critique, and scale effects.
- [2018 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2018ltr.pdf) - Marks the shift away from book value as the front-page scorecard and develops the American Tailwind theme.
- [2019 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2019ltr.pdf) - Useful for GAAP earnings noise after the accounting change requiring unrealized equity gains/losses in net income.
- [2021 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2021ltr.pdf) - Concise statement of business-picking, Berkshire's infrastructure base, and the four giants.
- [2022 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2022ltr.pdf) - Strong source for trust and rules, business-pickers, creative destruction, repurchase politics, and the flowers/weeds lesson.
- [2023 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2023ltr.pdf) - Buffett's Charlie Munger tribute and late-life restatement of Munger's role in moving Berkshire past Graham-only investing.
- [2024 Letter](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/2024ltr.pdf) - Late Buffett on cash versus equities, currency stability, controlled-business sale reluctance, and American capitalism's strengths and abuses.

### Interviews, speeches, meetings, and regulatory testimony

- [2010 FCIC Interview Transcript](https://fraser.stlouisfed.org/archival-collection/financial-crisis-inquiry-commission-4967/transcript-interview-warren-buffett-531019) - Official crisis-investigation interview; use for Moody's, due diligence, and what Buffett says he personally analyzed.
- [1998 University of Florida Lecture Transcript](https://tilsonfunds.com/BuffettUofFloridaspeech.pdf) - Transcript-grade source for Buffett teaching MBAs about circle of competence, careers, internet valuation, and compounding personal habits.

- [2017 Berkshire Annual Meeting Transcript](https://forum.valuepickr.com/uploads/default/original/2X/7/7e8e091d9fdbc77fd45d49479502f73a832bde4d.pdf) - Transcript source for Amazon/Google/Walmart omission discussion and late-stage technology-circle-of-competence evolution.
- [2020 Berkshire Annual Meeting Transcript](https://s3.amazonaws.com/static.contentres.com/media/documents/5c882ab5-3aaf-4f3d-ba33-d105bd25cc58.pdf) - Primary transcript for COVID-era airline-sale mistake, liquidity caution, and Buffett's explanation of whole-position exits.
- [2025 Berkshire Annual Meeting Transcript](https://steadycompounding.com/transcript/brk-2025/) - Secondary transcript of Buffett's final CEO-era meeting; useful for cash optionality, Japanese trading companies, succession, and utility-risk reassessment. Prefer an official transcript if one becomes available.

## Verification Notes and Dropped/Flagged Lines

- The popular line "price is what you pay; value is what you get" is included in Buffett's 2008 letter, but Buffett attributes it to Ben Graham. This file treats it as a Graham line repeated by Buffett, not a Buffett-origin quote.
- The common reputation line "It takes 20 years to build a reputation and five minutes to ruin it" was not included because this run did not locate a primary venue with sufficient context.
- The Salomon line about losing money versus losing reputation appears in video and press accounts, but this run did not locate a full official hearing transcript. It should be added later only after full-context verification.
- Quote aggregators, Goodreads pages, social posts, and listicles were used only as leads. They are not citation-grade for this file.
